Verb

A verb is a word that expresses an action, occurrence, or state of being. Verbs are essential in forming sentences, as they tell us what the subject is doing or experiencing.

Examples

He runs every morning.

She is a doctor.

They played cricket yesterday.

Types of Verbs

  1. Action Verbs – Show physical or mental actions.
    Example: run, jump, think, write
  2. Linking Verbs – Connect the subject with more information.
    Example: is, am, are, was, were, seem, become
  3. Helping (Auxiliary) Verbs – Help the main verb show tense, mood, or voice.
    Example: have, has, had, will, can, may, should
  4. Transitive Verbs – Require a direct object to complete their meaning.
    Example: She wrote a letter.
  5. Intransitive Verbs – Do not require a direct object.
    Example: He sleeps peacefully.
  6. Regular Verbs – Form past tense by adding “-ed”.
    Example: walk → walked, play → played
  7. Irregular Verbs – Change form completely in the past tense.
    Example: go → went, eat → ate

Verb Forms

  • Base Form (go, eat, write)
  • Past Simple (went, ate, wrote)
  • Past Participle (gone, eaten, written)
  • Present Participle / -ing Form (going, eating, writing)

Rules for Using Verbs

  • Verbs must agree with the subject in number and person.
    He runs vs They run
  • Tenses indicate the time of action (past, present, future).
  • Use auxiliary verbs to form continuous, perfect, or passive structures.

Examples in Sentences

She writes beautifully. (Action Verb)

They are happy. (Linking Verb)

I have finished my homework. (Helping Verb)

He reads books daily. (Present Tense)

We played football yesterday. (Past Tense)

Tips

  • Always check subject-verb agreement.
  • Choose the correct verb tense for clarity.
  • Remember irregular verbs don’t follow “-ed” rule in past tense.
