Adverb

An adverb is a word that modifies a verb, an adjective, or another adverb. It tells us more about how, when, where, or to what extent something happens.

Example: She runs quickly. ("quickly" describes how she runs)

Functions of Adverbs

  • Modify verbs: He speaks loudly.
  • Modify adjectives: This is very good.
  • Modify other adverbs: She runs quite fast.

Types of Adverbs

1. Adverbs of Manner

Describe how an action is performed.

Example: She danced beautifully.

2. Adverbs of Time

Tell us when something happens.

Example: He will arrive tomorrow.

3. Adverbs of Place

Tell us where something happens.

Example: The kids are playing outside.

4. Adverbs of Frequency

Tell us how often something happens.

Example: She always wakes up early.

5. Adverbs of Degree

Tell us the intensity or degree of something.

Example: This coffee is too hot.

Formation of Adverbs

Many adverbs are formed by adding -ly to adjectives.

  • Quick → Quickly
  • Beautiful → Beautifully
  • Careful → Carefully

However, not all adverbs end in -ly (e.g., fast, well, very).

Position of Adverbs in a Sentence

  • At the beginning: Yesterday, I went to the park.
  • Before the main verb: She always smiles.
  • After the main verb: He runs fast.
  • At the end: They played happily in the garden.

Common Mistakes

Don’t confuse adjectives with adverbs. She is a quick runner (adjective), but She runs quickly (adverb).
