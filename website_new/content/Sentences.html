Sentences

A sentence is a group of words that expresses a complete thought. It contains a subject and a predicate, and conveys a clear meaning.

Basic Structure

  • Subject – What or who the sentence is about.
  • Predicate – What is said about the subject.

Types of Sentences

  1. Declarative – Makes a statement.
    Example: The sun rises in the east.
  2. Interrogative – Asks a question.
    Example: Where are you going?
  3. Imperative – Gives a command or request.
    Example: Please close the door.
  4. Exclamatory – Expresses strong feelings.
    Example: What a beautiful day!

Rules for Writing Sentences

  • Begin with a capital letter.
  • End with appropriate punctuation (. ? !).
  • Include both subject and predicate.
  • Maintain correct word order: Subject + Verb + Object.

Examples

Correct: She loves reading books.

Incorrect: reading books she loves.

Tips

  • Keep sentences clear and concise.
  • Avoid run-on sentences by using proper punctuation.
  • Vary sentence types to make writing more engaging.
