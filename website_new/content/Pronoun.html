Pronoun

A pronoun is a word that takes the place of a noun in a sentence. Pronouns are used to avoid repeating nouns and make sentences shorter and clearer.

Examples

Instead of saying: Ravi is my friend. Ravi likes cricket.

We say: Ravi is my friend. He likes cricket.

Types of Pronouns

  1. Personal Pronouns – Used for specific people or things.
    Example: I, you, he, she, it, we, they
  2. Possessive Pronouns – Show ownership.
    Example: mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs
  3. Reflexive Pronouns – Refer back to the subject.
    Example: myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, themselves
  4. Demonstrative Pronouns – Point to specific things.
    Example: this, that, these, those
  5. Interrogative Pronouns – Used to ask questions.
    Example: who, whom, whose, which, what
  6. Relative Pronouns – Introduce relative clauses.
    Example: who, whom, whose, which, that
  7. Indefinite Pronouns – Refer to non-specific people or things.
    Example: someone, everyone, anything, nobody

Rules for Using Pronouns

  • Pronouns must agree with their antecedents in number and gender.
  • Use singular pronouns for singular nouns, plural for plural nouns.
  • Reflexive pronouns are used only when the subject and object are the same.

Examples in Sentences

She is my best friend. (Personal Pronoun)

This pen is mine. (Possessive Pronoun)

I made it myself. (Reflexive Pronoun)

These are my books. (Demonstrative Pronoun)

Who is calling? (Interrogative Pronoun)

Tips

  • Always make sure a pronoun clearly refers to a specific noun (antecedent).
  • Do not overuse pronouns without clear reference, as it may confuse readers.
  • Reflexive pronouns should never be used in place of subject or object pronouns.
