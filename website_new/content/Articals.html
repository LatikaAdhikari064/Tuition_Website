Articles in English Grammar

Articles are words used before nouns to define whether the noun is specific or general. In English, there are three articles:

  • A
  • An
  • The

1. Indefinite Articles – A & An

These are used when talking about something for the first time, or something that is not specific.

  • A – used before words starting with a consonant sound. Example: a cat, a house, a university (sounds like "you-niversity").
  • An – used before words starting with a vowel sound (a, e, i, o, u). Example: an apple, an hour (silent h), an honest man.

Rules for A/An:

  • Base it on sound, not spelling (e.g., "a European country" → 'yoo' sound).
  • Use when mentioning something for the first time.
  • Use with singular countable nouns.

2. Definite Article – The

This is used when referring to a specific person, place, or thing that the listener/reader already knows about.

  • The sun rises in the east.
  • The Taj Mahal is in Agra.
  • I saw the boy we met yesterday.

Rules for The:

  • Use with unique things (the moon, the Earth).
  • Use before superlatives (the best, the most beautiful).
  • Use when something is already mentioned earlier.
  • Use before names of seas, rivers, mountain ranges, newspapers.

3. Omission of Articles

Sometimes, no article is used. This is called "zero article".

  • Before plural countable nouns when talking in general: "Cats are cute."
  • Before uncountable nouns when speaking generally: "Milk is healthy."
  • Before names of countries, cities, languages: "I live in India." / "He speaks Spanish."
