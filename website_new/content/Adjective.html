Adjective

An adjective is a word that describes or modifies a noun or pronoun. It provides more information about the quality, quantity, size, shape, color, origin, or other characteristics of the noun.

Example: She is a beautiful girl. ("beautiful" describes the noun "girl")

Functions of Adjectives

  • Describe a noun: tall building, red car
  • Describe a pronoun: He is smart.
  • Show possession: my book, their house

Types of Adjectives

1. Descriptive Adjectives

Describe the quality or kind of a noun.

Example: The weather is pleasant.

2. Quantitative Adjectives

Tell us the quantity of a noun.

Example: I have three apples.

3. Demonstrative Adjectives

Point out specific nouns.

Example: This book is interesting.

4. Possessive Adjectives

Show ownership or possession.

Example: That is my pen.

5. Interrogative Adjectives

Used to ask questions about a noun.

Example: Which subject do you like?

6. Proper Adjectives

Derived from proper nouns and indicate origin or nationality.

Example: He is an Indian player.

Position of Adjectives

  • Before the noun: She has a big house.
  • After the verb (linking verb): The food is delicious.

Order of Adjectives

When multiple adjectives are used, the usual order is:

  • Opinion → Size → Age → Shape → Color → Origin → Material → Purpose + Noun
Example: A beautiful (opinion), big (size), old (age), round (shape), wooden (material) table.

Degrees of Comparison

  • Positive Degree: She is tall.
  • Comparative Degree: She is taller than me.
  • Superlative Degree: She is the tallest in the class.

Common Mistakes

Don’t use double comparatives or superlatives: ❌ more better → ✅ better ❌ most tallest → ✅ tallest
