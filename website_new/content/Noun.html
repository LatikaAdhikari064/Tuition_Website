Noun

A noun is a word used to identify a person, place, thing, or idea. It can function as the subject or object of a verb, or as the object of a preposition.

Examples

Person: teacher, doctor, John

Place: school, park, India

Thing: book, car, phone

Idea: freedom, love, happiness

Types of Nouns

  1. Proper Nouns – Names of specific people, places, or things.
    Example: London, Microsoft, Mahatma Gandhi
  2. Common Nouns – General names for people, places, or things.
    Example: city, company, leader
  3. Concrete Nouns – Things you can see, touch, hear, smell, or taste.
    Example: apple, dog, music
  4. Abstract Nouns – Ideas, qualities, or states that cannot be touched.
    Example: honesty, beauty, courage
  5. Collective Nouns – Words that name a group.
    Example: team, flock, jury
  6. Countable Nouns – Things you can count.
    Example: apples, chairs, pens
  7. Uncountable Nouns – Things you cannot count.
    Example: water, sugar, information

Rules for Using Nouns

  • Proper nouns always begin with a capital letter.
  • Countable nouns can be singular or plural.
  • Uncountable nouns do not take plural forms.
  • Articles (a, an, the) are often used before nouns.

Examples in Sentences

The teacher is writing on the board. (Common Noun)

India is a beautiful country. (Proper Noun)

We should value honesty. (Abstract Noun)

Tips

  • Identify whether a noun is countable or uncountable before using articles.
  • Use singular verbs with singular nouns, plural verbs with plural nouns.
  • Remember collective nouns may take singular or plural verbs depending on context.
