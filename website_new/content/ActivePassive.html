Active and Passive Voice

What is Voice in Grammar?

In English grammar, voice refers to the form of a verb that indicates whether the subject of the sentence is performing the action (Active Voice) or receiving the action (Passive Voice).

Active Voice

In the Active Voice, the subject performs the action expressed by the verb. This form is direct, clear, and usually preferred in writing.

Example: The teacher (subject) explains (verb) the lesson (object).

Passive Voice

In the Passive Voice, the subject receives the action. The focus is on the object of the action rather than the doer.

Example: The lesson (subject) is explained (verb) by the teacher (agent).

Structure

Active Voice Structure

Subject + Verb + Object

Passive Voice Structure

Object + Form of "to be" + Past Participle + (by + Subject)

When to Use Passive Voice?

  • When the doer is unknown.
  • When the doer is unimportant.
  • When you want to emphasize the action or the receiver of the action.

Examples

Active to Passive Conversion

  • Active: She wrote a letter. → Passive: A letter was written by her.
  • Active: They are building a new bridge. → Passive: A new bridge is being built by them.
  • Active: The chef cooked the meal. → Passive: The meal was cooked by the chef.
